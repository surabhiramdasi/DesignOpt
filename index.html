Topology Optimization

Topology optimization is a powerful tool that can be used to expedite the ALD process. The design-analysis iterative process for complex components like cylinder heads, flywheel housings can easily take months, if not years. Also, some of the high stress, high deformation areas are not very intuitive to fix. Additionally, the typical approach to fix a lot of design flaws is to add material to the structure. This can easily make the components a whole lot heavier than they need to be. Topology optimization helps reduce the number of analysis and design iterations and can also be effectively used to reduce the weight of the components - only if the analyst ensures the correct loading and boundary conditions. Topology optimization is typically used for simple components like lifting brackets, other accessory brackets since the loading and boundary conditions are straightforward. I was able to use topology optimization for a cylinder head to effectively improve the stiffness of the whole structure. Almost 75% of the stress locations showed major improvements after implementing the suggestions from the topology optimization results. In addition to that, the new cylinder head design was ~10 kgs lighter than the baseline product. Also, these changes helped fixed problematic areas which were already iterated on at least 15-20 times using the traditional design-analysis iterative approach. Ansys topology optimization and VR&D Genesis are my preferred tools for any topology optimization projects.
